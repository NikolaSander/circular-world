The Global Flow of People

In a paper published in Science (free access) in March 2014, Guy Abel and Nikola Sander quantify and visualise global international migration flows for the first time. The Global Flow of People shows migration flows between 196 countries over 5-year periods since 1990.

Internal Migration in Germany

We visualise data on inter-regional migration in Germany, which has been published by Nikola Sander in Comparative Population Studies. Our visualisation puts East-West migration into perspective and highlights a new re-urbanisation trend.

Global Migration & Income

This visualisation of the flow estimates published by Guy Abel and Nikola Sander in Science is not interactive, but it's embedded into a very nice story by Marc Fischetti in Scientific American on how people move up the income ladder in steps.

Next

Humanity's Cultural History

An interactive visualisation of humanity's cultural history over two millennia. Here we use data published by Maximilian Schich and colleagues to trace the places of birth and death of notable people in Europe and North America since year 1 CE.
