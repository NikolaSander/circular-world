The Global Flow of People

In a paper published in Science (free access here) in March 2014, we quantify and visualise global international migration flows for the first time. The Global Flow of People shows migration flows between 196 countries over 5-year periods since 1990.

Internal Migration in Germany

We visualise data on inter-regional migration in Germany, which has been published in Comparative Population Studies. Our dataviz puts East-West migration into perspective and highlights a new re-urbanisation trend.

Next

Global Migration & Income Levels

Our new flow estimates published in Science let us visualise the flow of people from middle- to high-income countries. This visualisation in Scientific American reveals that it's not the poorest who migrate the most.

Next

Humanity's Cultural History

An interactive visualisation of humanity's cultural history over two millennia. We use data published by Maximilian Schich and colleagues to trace the places of birth and death of notable people across space and time.
